v3a: Reversal Rejection

Designed to capture "bounces" off significant Support or Resistance levels. It looks for price rejection where the previous move was exhausting.

Entry Conditions (BUY)

  • Price currently closed ABOVE a focused SR bucket.
  • The 1-period previous candle was BEARISH (Red), indicating a rejection move.
  • Wait for the confirmation candle to close.

Logic Flow

graph TD A[New H1 Bar] --> B{Price > SR Level?} B -- Yes --> C{Previous Candle Red?} B -- No --> D{Price < SR Level?} C -- Yes --> E[OPEN BUY MARKET] D -- Yes --> F{Previous Candle Green?} F -- Yes --> G[OPEN SELL MARKET] C -- No --> H[Discard Signal]

Implementation Note

Utilizes FindFocusSR() to detect which level is currently being "tapped." This avoids execution on stale levels or irrelevant price action.

v3b: Follow-Trend Confirmation

A continuation strategy that anticipates a breakthrough or a strong departure from an SR level after a touch-and-go scenario.

Entry Conditions (BUY)

  • Price currently closed ABOVE a focused SR bucket.
  • The 1-period previous candle was BULLISH (Green), showing momentum starting from the level.
  • Strong confirmation of trend persistence.

Logic Flow

graph TD A[New H1 Bar] --> B{Price/SR Check} B -- "Price > SR" --> C{Prev Candle Green?} C -- "Yes" --> E[OPEN BUY MARKET] B -- "Price < SR" --> D{Prev Candle Red?} D -- "Yes" --> G[OPEN SELL MARKET] C -- "No" --> H[Wait] D -- "No" --> H

v3c: Center-Triggered Stop Orders

This strategy operates between two SR levels. It uses the "Center Point" as a trigger sensitivity zone to place breakout orders at the boundaries.

Entry Conditions

  • Price crosses the Center Line (High SR + Low SR) / 2.
  • Previous bar must have touched either the Upper or Lower SR.
  • Action: Places two STOP ORDERS (Buy Stop above Resistance, Sell Stop below Support).

Logic Flow

graph TD Cross[Center Line Crossed] --> Touch{Touched SR?} Touch -- Yes --> Setup[Place Buy-Stop & Sell-Stop] Setup --> Wait[Wait for Fill or 24h Expiry] Touch -- No --> Discard[Ignore Signal]

Parametric Validity

Uses validity = dist / 6.0 to calculate the ideal offset for order placement, ensuring orders aren't triggered by noise at the level itself.

v3d: Center-Triggered Limit Orders

A "Fading" strategy that expects price to stay range-bound. It bets on the SR levels holding firm as boundaries.

Entry Conditions

  • Price crosses the Center Line of the current SR zone.
  • Previous bar must have touched a boundary level.
  • Action: Places two LIMIT ORDERS (Buy Limit at Support, Sell Limit at Resistance).

Workflow

graph LR Cross[Center Crossed] --> Type{Touched SR?} Type -- Yes --> Orders[Place Buy & Sell Limit] Orders --> Wait[Wait for TP or 24h Overtime]

Global Universal Parameters

InpRatio (1.0) Reward to Risk ratio. TP Dist = SL Dist * Ratio.
InpOvertime (24h) Maximum time a trade can remain open before being forced closed.
InpSigTol (0.5) Tick tolerance for level touch detection.
InpPollInterval (60s) Frequency of level updates from the REST API.
